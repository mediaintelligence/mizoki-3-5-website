Governance Framework

The Decision Control Plane enforces governance at the architectural level—agents cannot bypass it.

Decision Control Plane (DCP) Governance

Every autonomous proposal must pass through the DCP. The system supports five authorization outcomes:

  • Approve: Execute as proposed when all thresholds are met
  • Modify: Adjust parameters to bring within acceptable bounds
  • Defer: Await additional validation or simulation results
  • Reject: Block proposals that fail confidence or policy thresholds
  • Escalate: Route to human review for high-risk or novel situations

Policy-Driven Autonomy

Define what can be automated, what requires approval, and what must remain advisory. Policies are versioned and auditable.

Risk thresholds • Dollar limits • Domain restrictions • Time windows

Role-Based Access Control

Granular permissions for approve, execute, audit, and configure. Separation of duties enforced at the platform level.

RBAC • SSO integration • Activity logging • Session management
