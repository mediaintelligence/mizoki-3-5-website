Decision Control Plane

Proposal ≠ Authorization.

The DCP separates reasoning from execution authority. No autonomous action occurs without verification, simulation, policy clearance, risk arbitration, and causal eligibility.

Foundation of governed enterprise autonomy
Verification & Arbitration

Disagreement is intelligence.

Independent Planner, Verifier, Risk, Policy, Simulation, and Compliance agents continuously evaluate proposed actions. The system measures confidence divergence, causal inconsistency, and hallucination probability before any authorization.

Output agreement metrics, not raw confidence
Counterfactual Simulation

Reason against what could have been.

Every consequential decision is simulated against alternative actions, delayed actions, no-action baselines, adversarial scenarios, regulatory shifts, and market volatility — creating compounding strategic intelligence.

Engine for compounding learning
Decision Eligibility Layer

Refuses to act on correlation alone.

DEL programmatically prevents autonomous action based on correlation alone. Only causally grounded, policy-cleared, simulation-verified, risk-authorized decisions can become autonomous. This is the difference between predictive automation and governed strategic autonomy.
