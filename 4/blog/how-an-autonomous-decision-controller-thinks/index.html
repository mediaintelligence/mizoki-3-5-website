How an Autonomous Decision Controller Thinks

Boris Mizhen · 10 min read · April 2026 · DOC. MZK3-J-HOW-

Strip away the vocabulary and every disciplined autonomous decision has the same skeleton. In MIZOKI3 that skeleton is explicit — seven stages, one governed pathway, no shortcuts. Here is the loop in plain English.

Sense

Reality streams in continuously — telemetry, documents, ledger events — and lands in a temporal-causal knowledge graph. Not rows in tables: relationships with history. The system's first job is simply to have an accurate, current model of the organization.

Reason & Plan

When something changes, the system reasons over the graph: what does this mean, what could be done, and what are the second-order effects? A candidate plan is sequenced with rollback points, and every domain the action touches is flagged as a stakeholder up front.

Validate

The plan is run as a counterfactual simulation against every binding constraint — covenants, contracts, policies, regulatory frameworks. This is the stage most systems skip and the stage that makes the rest trustworthy.

Decide

An eligibility score is computed per decision, per domain. Above the threshold, the action is eligible for autonomous execution; below it, the Decision Control Plane vetoes and re-routes. Deterministic policy and human-in-the-loop keys sit at this gate.

Act

Only the authorized action executes — scoped, reversible, bounded, and written to a tamper-evident trail as it happens.

Learn

The full trace becomes training material for the system itself: promoted into reusable skills so the same class of situation is recognized faster next time. The loop doesn't just execute decisions. It compounds judgment.

Sense → Reason → Plan → Validate → Decide → Act → Learn. Every signal. One pathway. No exceptions.
