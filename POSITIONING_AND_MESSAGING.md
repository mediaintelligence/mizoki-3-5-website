## Positioning & Messaging (CANONICAL — read before writing any product copy, UI labels, or docs)

This rule is shared across the website repo and this platform repo. Both must agree.

The metaphor is a **nervous system**, never a "brain." MIZOKI3 gives a business a
real-time, mathematical understanding of *every* part of itself — a living graph of
metrics, relationships, and **prediction**. It senses the whole organization at once
and acts the moment something changes. It **replaces the CRM and the linear,
backwards-looking analytics stack** (which only record what already happened) with a
forward-looking, predictive system.

**Say:** nervous system · real-time understanding · living/mathematical graph of
metrics and prediction · senses across the whole organization · adapts to your
business · replaces the CRM and backwards-looking dashboards · reflex arc (for the
SRPVDAL loop).

**Never say:** "brain," "one brain," "the brain of your business." No "Living Brain"
visualizer language.

**Divisions / domains / cells are NOT the product.** The system is unlimited and
adaptive — it grows a decision controller for whatever domains a business actually has
(3, 40, or entirely its own). The five lenses (Counsel / Estate / Capital / Signal /
Risk) are **only the structures of customers we've onboarded so far** — they are
*example deployments*, not a fixed offering or a list to fit into. This applies to
runtime/agent surfaces too: subagent rosters, console sidebars, and any cell taxonomy
should be presented as configurable per tenant, never as the canonical shape of the
product.

Canonical hero line: **"A nervous system for your business."**
