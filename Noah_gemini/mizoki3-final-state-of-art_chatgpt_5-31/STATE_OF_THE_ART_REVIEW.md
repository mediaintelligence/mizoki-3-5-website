# MIZOKI3 State-of-the-Art Site Review

## 1. Does the homepage immediately feel more enterprise-grade?

Yes. The homepage now leads with enterprise-grade infrastructure language:
**Enterprise action governed by causality.**

State-of-the-art improvement:
- Make the hero feel like an operating environment, not a marketing page.
- Show causal intelligence, authority status, DCP enforcement, and TCKG status immediately.

## 2. Does the site explain why MIZOKI3 is not just another AI dashboard?

Yes. The Anti-Dashboard Thesis makes this explicit.

State-of-the-art improvement:
- Dashboards observe.
- MIZOKI3 authorizes governed action.
- The site now makes that distinction concrete.

## 3. Does the simulator make the product feel real?

Yes. The simulator is now the strongest product proof.

State-of-the-art improvement:
- Use Capital, Media, and Counsel scenarios.
- Add risk/policy slider.
- Add counterfactual paths.
- Add stage-by-stage SRPVDAL execution.
- Add agent arbitration and terminal audit logs.

## 4. Does the Decision Control Plane feel like the AI governor?

Yes. The DCP now acts as the trust object.

State-of-the-art improvement:
- Treat the DCP like an authorization firewall.
- Show context, identity, policy, exposure, escalation, and execution status.

## 5. Does the SRPVDAL loop feel understandable and visual?

Yes. It appears as an interactive engine and as the simulator execution sequence.

State-of-the-art improvement:
- Repeat the loop in multiple contexts:
  - Engine page
  - Simulator
  - Replay
  - Governance
  - Story arc

## 6. Does the site clearly say AI should not act unless the right conditions are true?

Yes. This is now the core brand thesis.

State-of-the-art improvement:
- Make this sentence the moat:
  **The question is not whether AI can act. The question is what must be true before it is allowed to act.**

## 7. Does the visual system feel premium, institutional, and credible?

Yes. It now uses a restrained command-center visual language.

State-of-the-art improvement:
- Every visual must prove one of four things:
  - causality
  - authorization
  - traceability
  - learning
